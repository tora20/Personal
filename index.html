Virtual Reality

I love engaging in Virtual Reality activities that are at the nexus of Psychology. When designing a 3D virtual environment, a developer should consider how sensory perception, lighting and other stimuli play a role in a user's experience. Some simulations that have inspired me in the past were a 3D environment that immerses people to a setting at high heights, in an attempt to help the user overcome their fear of heights. I also am intigued by a project by the US AirForce Academy to train people to withstand some possible scenarios that might occur while on duty. I understand also that VR can help with OCD.

Personally, I have enjoyed conducting research in my Introduction to Psychology class about how we can modify VR environments by presenting and obscuring certain stimuli, and how that affects attention and learning for students with ADHD. Sometimes it is the smallest modification that can make the biggest difference!

My goals in the future are to bring dance to the VR world, so that people can connect from all over the world to learn dances from different cultures with one another. I especially desired to have an app like this during covid, when my friends and I were all sent home for remote learning. I was a part of a KPop dance club that would meet up to learn dances off youtube and film them together, but we could no longer do this remotely. My solution to the problem: create a VR environemnt with a virtual dance mirror that allows friends to meet up virtually in a designated dance room, take lessons virtually, play their favorite songs and gain spatial feedback while dancing with one another! I am inspired to make dance lessons accessible to everyone, especially while in person ones are expensive.
